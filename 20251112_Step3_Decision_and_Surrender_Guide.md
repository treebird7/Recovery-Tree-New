# STEP 3: DECISION & SURRENDER - INTRODUCTION GUIDE
## Recovery Tree Project

**Date Created:** 2025-11-12  
**Purpose:** Guide user from believing recovery is possible (Step 2) to deciding to accept help and turn will/life over to Higher Power

---

## OVERVIEW

Step 3 is about **decision** - choosing to stop running the show alone.

After believing help exists (Step 2), Step 3 is where you actually accept it. You decide to let something other than your willpower guide you.

**The decision:** "We made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we understood Him."

This isn't a feeling. It's not proof. It's not a guarantee. It's a choice: **"I'm willing to try this differently. I'm willing to let go of control and accept help."**

---

## STRUCTURE: FROM BELIEF TO DECISION

### Phase 1: UNDERSTANDING THE DECISION
What does "turning it over" actually mean?

### Phase 2: EXAMINING RESISTANCE
What stops you from letting go of control?

### Phase 3: CLARIFYING YOUR HIGHER POWER
Being specific about what you're turning things over to

### Phase 4: MAKING THE DECISION
The actual Step 3 moment - saying yes

---

## ELDER TREE VOICE GUIDELINES FOR STEP 3

**Tone shifts from Step 2:**
- Step 2 was opening to possibility
- Step 3 is choosing to act on that possibility

**Key principles:**
- This is about willingness, not perfection
- The decision comes before the feeling
- They'll "grab control back" - that's normal
- One decision, practiced daily
- Focus on what they're deciding, not how to do it perfectly

**Effective phrases:**
- "What would change if you actually tried this?"
- "What are you afraid of losing if you let go?"
- "This is a decision, not a promise to never struggle again"
- "You're not deciding to be perfect - just willing to try"

**Avoid:**
- "Just let go and let God" (too vague)
- Promising it will be easy
- Implying they're doing it wrong if they struggle
- Making it sound like one-time event rather than daily practice

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Session Length
- Shortest of Steps 1-3 (20-45 minutes typical)
- This is decision, not excavation
- Can be done in one sitting

### Common Resistance Points
- "What if I do it wrong?" - Emphasize practice over perfection
- "What if nothing changes?" - This is first step of change, not instant transformation
- "I don't know how" - The HOW comes in steps 4-12, this is just the decision
- "I'm not ready" - Explore what "ready" would look like

### Emotional Tone
- Step 1: Heavy truth
- Step 2: Opening to hope
- Step 3: Relief of surrender (but also fear)
- Watch for resistance masking as confusion

### After Step 3
- User has made decision to surrender control
- Has identified their Higher Power concept
- Knows their daily practice for turning things over
- Ready for Step 4 (seeing what blocks them from this)

---

## CRITICAL BRIDGE TO STEP 4

**Important Context:**

Most people need to LIVE Step 3 for a while before they're ready for Step 4. They need to:
- Practice turning things over
- Notice when they grab control back
- Experience the gap between their decision and their automatic reactions
- Get frustrated that they can't seem to stay surrendered

**The Step 4 invitation comes when they ask:** "Why do I keep grabbing control back? What's stopping me from living this decision?"

Answer: "You can't turn over what you can't see. Step 4 is where you see it clearly."

Use the transition prompt (20251026_Step3-to-Step4_Transition_Prompt.md) when users have been working Step 3 for 7-14+ days and showing signs of this gap.
